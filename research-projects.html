Active Research Projects

Gene and Regulatory Sequence Identification using Communications Theory
Completed 2025
Period: 2023-2025
Role: Principal Investigator
Type: Start-up Grant (UAEU)
This pioneering project applies advanced communications theory, coding theory, and information theory principles to identify genes and regulatory sequences in genomic data. By treating DNA sequences as communication channels, the research develops novel algorithms for detecting protein-coding regions, transcription factor binding sites, and other regulatory elements with improved accuracy and efficiency.
Funding: 367,000 AED
Offline Assistive System with Stereo Vision for Visually Impaired
Active
Period: 2025-2026
Role: Supervisor
Type: SURE Plus Grant
Development of an innovative offline assistive system utilizing stereo vision technology to aid visually impaired individuals. The system employs computer vision algorithms and machine learning to provide real-time environmental awareness and obstacle detection without requiring internet connectivity.
Funding: 40,000 AED
Innovative Sight-Assist Glasses with CNN-LSTM Architecture
Completed 2025
Period: 2024-2025
Role: Supervisor
Type: SURE Plus Grant
Smart assistive glasses incorporating Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks for real-time scene understanding and object recognition. The system provides audio feedback to help visually impaired users navigate their environment safely and independently.
Funding: 40,000 AED
Assistive Smart Glasses for Visually Impaired
Completed 2023
Period: Feb-May 2023
Role: Supervisor

Initial prototype of smart glasses designed to assist visually impaired individuals through computer vision and audio feedback systems. This project laid the foundation for subsequent advanced assistive technology research.
